Descirbe the text

"Bad Indians" is a memoir written by Deborah Miranda, a former WLU professor. The book focuses on Miranda's family history, her personal experiences, and her ancestral Californian Indigenous communities. Miranda uses a non-linear and fragmented narrative style, incorporating different voices and perspectives.

What container is it in? Is that container unique or one of many copies?

The container is a book, although inside, it is formatted uniquely, including pictures and a little bit of history of Indigenous people. The container itself is not unique, as there are many copies.

Is it published? How can you access the text? Can you buy it or check it out of the library? Print or digital or both? What other forms might exist?

It is published by "Heyday," a publishing company. You can access this book in a variety of ways. The first time I was introduced to the text was in a PDF in my poverty studies class when my professor told us to read it because I was going to do a presentation on it. It is available both digitally and in print, and it can be bought or checked out from a library. In 50 years, people can still read it because it will be available in print and digitally.

How did this text come to be?

I am not sure about how this text came to be, but I know it is deeply personal, and she probably made it to share her story so others can see the intersectionality of being a poor Indigenous woman.

Could it be studied by a scholar?

As I mentioned before, as this book explores the intersectionality of being a poor minority woman, it can definitely be studied by scholars.
